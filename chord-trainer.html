Field notes

01  This mode teaches shapes and changes, not perfect voicing. It listens for a strum and checks the right notes are ringing — it won't nitpick one buzzy string.

02  Self-paced: the highlighted chord waits for you. Fret the shape, strum once, let it ring — then move your hand to the next.

03  Chords that share notes (C and Am, say) can both pass — that's expected in ballpark mode. Slide the easy ↔ strict control to taste.

04  Ukulele shapes are hand-tuned; guitar too. Banjo shapes are auto-voiced, and piano lights up the chord tones. All four hear the same way.

05  Hear this chord and Play the song pause the judging so the speaker doesn't play the song for you.

06  Rhythm: plain ChordPro carries no timing, so a chord line that's just lyrics gets one strum per chord. For rhythm practice, write a slash line — | [C] / / / | [G7] / / / | — and each chord shows real slash notation, one strum per beat. Add {time: 4/4} up top. (Self-paced mode still won't grade your timing — that's what a future metronome mode is for — but the beats are now in the chart.)
